FAQs

Frequently asked questions

Everything about how Kraken distributes.

How does Kraken connect to Robinhood?›

Kraken links to the Robinhood account that holds the ETF positions using a secure, read-and-distribute connection. It syncs live positions to size each holder's share.

Why every 3 minutes?›

The 3-minute cadence keeps distributions frequent and predictable without overwhelming the account. It's the default clock, and every cycle is guaranteed to run.

How is each holder's share calculated?›

Allocation is deterministic and proportional to a holder's stake in the registry. The same inputs always produce the same split, so every payout is reproducible.

What happens if a cycle fails?›

The engine automatically retries and logs the event. Cycle guarantees mean a missed window is caught and reconciled on the next run.

Can I audit past distributions?›

Yes. Every distribution is timestamped and exportable — amount, recipient, and cycle — so the full history is auditable end to end.
